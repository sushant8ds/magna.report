\chapter{INTRODUCTION}

\justify

The manufacturing industry demands zero-defect quality assurance, particularly in safety-critical automotive components. High-Pressure Die Casting (HPDC) is a widely used process for producing complex aluminium structural parts at high production rates. Despite its efficiency, HPDC introduces surface and sub-surface defects --- most critically \textbf{Cracks} and \textbf{Cold Shuts} --- that pose structural safety risks if left undetected. Manual visual inspection is labour-intensive, subjective, and impractical at production scale.

This project, developed in partnership with Magna International, builds an automated deep learning pipeline to detect and localise surface defects in HPDC castings using RF-DETR (Roboflow Detection Transformer), a state-of-the-art object detection model released under the Apache 2.0 licence. The system is specifically engineered to handle the extreme small-object detection challenge inherent to crack inspection, where crack bounding boxes occupy less than 0.1\% of the total image area.
