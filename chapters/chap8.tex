\justify

\textbf{Experiment 1 --- Baseline (768 Patches):} Training on 3,460 patches with no balancing or augmentation achieved EMA mAP50-95 = 0.2144. Bottlenecks: 80.2\% negative patches and boundary truncation of cracks.

\textbf{Experiment 4 --- 768 Balanced + Augmented:} Dataset balancing + horizontal flip on crack patches improved Crack AP by $+$17.4\% (0.154 $\to$ 0.181) and overall EMA mAP to 0.2157, confirming dataset quality as the primary driver.

\textbf{Experiment 7 --- 1024 Patches with 35\% Overlap (Current Best):} Scaling patches to 1024 px with 35\% overlap produced the largest gain. The positive ratio improved naturally to 31.98\% without explicit balancing.

\textbf{Key findings:}
\begin{enumerate}[leftmargin=2em]
    \item Dataset engineering outperforms architecture changes --- all improvement came from data pipeline changes with identical model architecture.
    \item Patch size is the most impactful variable: 768 $\to$ 1024 px with 35\% overlap gave the largest single jump ($+$10.3\% EMA mAP50-95).
    \item Crack-focused augmentation alone gave $+$17.4\% Crack AP in Experiment 4.
    \item EMA checkpoint is consistently superior across all experiments.
\end{enumerate}

\chapter{CONCLUSIONS AND FUTURE SCOPE}

\justify

\section*{Conclusions}

This project successfully developed and validated an automated deep learning pipeline for surface defect detection in HPDC aluminium castings. The following were achieved in Phase 1:

\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=2em]
    \item RF-DETR validated as a commercially deployable alternative (Apache 2.0) to YOLO-family models.
    \item A patch-based training pipeline converting 256 full images into 4,184 training patches at 1024$\times$1024 resolution.
    \item EMA mAP50-95 of \textbf{0.2379} --- an $\approx$11\% improvement over baseline --- achieved entirely through dataset engineering.
    \item Patch size scaling (768 $\to$ 1024 px, 35\% overlap) identified as the single most impactful improvement.
\end{itemize}

\begin{resultbox}[Final Best Configuration]
\begin{tabular}{ll}
Model:              & RF-DETR Medium (Apache 2.0) \\
Patch size:         & 1024 $\times$ 1024 px, 35\% overlap \\
Augmentation:       & Horizontal Flip (crack patches only) \\
EMA mAP50-95:       & \textbf{0.2379} \quad mAP50: \textbf{0.4647} \\
Checkpoint:         & \texttt{checkpoint\_best\_ema.pth} \\
\end{tabular}
\end{resultbox}

\section*{Future Scope}

\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=2em]
    \item \textbf{RF-DETR Large:} Evaluate larger backbone on the same 1024 dataset, expected $+$0.01--0.03 mAP gain.
    \item \textbf{Extended augmentation:} Vertical flip, 180° rotation, brightness jitter to delay overfitting.
    \item \textbf{50\% overlap:} Further reduce boundary truncation.
    \item \textbf{Phase 2 --- Crack Length Measurement:} Instance segmentation $\to$ Zhang-Suen skeletonisation $\to$ centerline extraction $\to$ crack length in mm.
    \item \textbf{Production integration:} Embed inference into Magna's production line quality control systems with a defect reporting dashboard.
\end{itemize}
